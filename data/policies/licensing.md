### LIC-1. Jurisdictional Licensing Requirement

The firm may only offer regulated financial products in jurisdictions where it holds an
active license or an applicable exemption. Sales or marketing to a customer located in
an unlicensed jurisdiction is prohibited, even if the customer initiated contact.

### LIC-2. Marketing Material Sign-Off

All external marketing material (ads, landing pages, emailed promotions) that references
rates, returns, or regulated products must be reviewed and signed off by Legal before
publication. Performance claims must be substantiated and include required risk
disclosures.

### LIC-3. Outside Business Activities

Employees must disclose and obtain approval from Legal/Compliance before engaging in any
outside business activity that could compete with, or create a conflict of interest
with, the firm's licensed activities.

### LIC-4. Referral and Introducer Arrangements

Paying a referral fee to an unlicensed third party for introducing clients into a
regulated product is prohibited unless the arrangement has been reviewed and approved by
Legal and, where required, registered with the relevant regulator.

### LIC-5. Cross-Border Service Provision

Providing services to a customer who has relocated to a jurisdiction where the firm is
not licensed requires a case-by-case Legal review; continuing to service the account
without review is a licensing violation, regardless of when the relationship began.

### LIC-6. New Product Launch Review

Before any new product or feature is launched, Legal must confirm it fits within the
scope of existing licenses, or that a new license/registration has been obtained.
Product and engineering teams may not launch a regulated feature without this sign-off.
