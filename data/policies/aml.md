### AML-1. Sanctions Screening — General Rule

Every new customer, and every counterparty on an outbound payment, must be screened
against the current OFAC, UN, and EU sanctions lists before onboarding or payment
release. A screening match of any confidence level must be manually reviewed by the AML
team before the relationship or payment proceeds. Screening may never be skipped for
speed or client relationship reasons.

### AML-2. Know Your Customer (KYC) Requirements

Individual customers require government-issued photo ID and proof of address dated
within 90 days. Business customers additionally require beneficial-ownership disclosure
for any individual owning 25% or more of the entity. Accounts may not be activated with
incomplete KYC documentation.

### AML-3. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

Customers identified as Politically Exposed Persons, or their immediate family members,
require Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) and sign-off from the AML Officer before
onboarding. PEP status must be re-screened at least annually for existing customers.

### AML-4. Transaction Monitoring Thresholds

Single transactions over $10,000, or cumulative transactions exceeding $10,000 within a
rolling 24-hour period for one customer, are automatically flagged for AML review.
Flagged transactions are held pending review and must not be released without AML
sign-off.

### AML-5. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

Any activity reasonably suspected of being linked to money laundering, terrorist
financing, or fraud must be escalated to the AML Officer for a Suspicious Activity
Report determination. Staff must not disclose to the customer that a SAR is being
considered or filed ("tipping off" is prohibited).

### AML-6. Sanctioned Jurisdiction Exposure

The firm does not knowingly onboard customers, or process payments, connected to
comprehensively sanctioned jurisdictions (e.g. as designated by OFAC's country-based
sanctions programs). Any application with an address, IP, or beneficial owner tied to
such a jurisdiction must be escalated to the AML Officer, not auto-approved.

### AML-7. Employee Conflicts and Gifts (AML-adjacent)

Employees involved in onboarding or transaction approval may not accept gifts over $100
from a customer or counterparty, and must disclose any personal relationship with a
customer under review to the AML Officer before proceeding.
