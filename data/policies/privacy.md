### PRIV-1. Customer Record Retention Period

Customer personal data (name, contact details, account history) must be retained for no
longer than 7 years after the account is closed, unless a longer period is required by
tax or securities law for the relevant jurisdiction. After the retention period expires,
records must be deleted or irreversibly anonymized within 90 days.

### PRIV-2. Cross-Border Data Transfers — General Rule

Personal data of EU/EEA data subjects may not leave the EEA unless an approved transfer
safeguard is in place: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), an adequacy decision, or
Binding Corporate Rules. A transfer without one of these safeguards is a policy
violation regardless of business justification.

### PRIV-3. EU Customer Data Hosted Outside the EEA

By default, EU customer personal data must be stored in EU-West region infrastructure.
Any exception that stores or processes EU customer data in a non-EEA region (including
US regions) requires: (a) an approved SCC on file for the receiving entity, and (b)
written sign-off from the Data Protection Officer prior to go-live. This is a high-stakes
change and must not be approved informally.

### PRIV-4. Consent and Purpose Limitation

Personal data may only be used for the purpose disclosed to the data subject at
collection. Using existing customer data for a new purpose (e.g. a new analytics
feature) requires either a documented legitimate-interest assessment or fresh consent,
reviewed by the DPO.

### PRIV-5. Data Subject Access & Deletion Requests

Data subject access requests (DSARs) and deletion requests must be fulfilled within 30
calendar days of verified receipt. Requests should be routed to the DPO's request queue
immediately upon identification; do not respond with data exports without identity
verification.

### PRIV-6. Data Breach Notification

Any suspected personal-data breach must be reported to the DPO within 24 hours of
discovery. Breaches meeting a regulatory notification threshold (likely risk to data
subjects) must be reported to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of
the organization becoming aware.

### PRIV-7. Vendor / Sub-Processor Data Sharing

Sharing personal data with a third-party vendor or sub-processor requires a signed Data
Processing Agreement (DPA) and an entry in the sub-processor register before any data is
transferred. Marketing or product teams may not share customer lists with a new vendor
without DPO sign-off.
